The Solar System

The Solar System is a majestic collection of worlds, each orbiting our brilliant star — the Sun. At its heart, the Sun radiates light and heat, sustaining life and driving the rhythms of the planets that surround it.

Closest to the Sun is Mercury, a scorched, cratered world that spins slowly through extreme temperatures. Venus, blanketed by thick clouds, shines brightly in our sky but hides a surface hot enough to melt lead. Earth, our home, is the only known planet to support life, with oceans, continents, and a delicate atmosphere.

Just beyond lies Mars, the red planet, with vast deserts and the largest volcano in the solar system. Jupiter, the giant, dwarfs all other planets with its swirling storms and dozens of moons. Saturn captivates with its iconic rings — icy, elegant, and enormous.

The outer planets, Uranus and Neptune, are cold and distant. Uranus spins on its side with a bluish glow, while Neptune howls with supersonic winds, guarding the edges of our cosmic neighborhood.
